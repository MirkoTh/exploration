Information on tasks and purpose of the study

Today, you are stepping into the role of a gambler visiting a series of casinos.
In each casino you are playing on a pair of slot machines.
Sometimes, a friend who wants to play as well joins and asks you about the payoffs you have received from the two machines.
Your goals are to gain as many rewards as possible and to remember the rewards returned by each of the slot machines and inform your friend about them.

The purpose of the study is to gain knowledge about how people choose between uncertain options.
A decision between uncertain options supposedly happens often in everyday life. An example could be to decide between dining in two different restaurants.
You may like the daily menues of one restaurant more than those of another restaurant.
But depending on the weekday and on the chef preparing the dishes you may expect some variation in food quality.
Even though you expect the food in one of the restaurants to be better, on some days the reverse is going to be true.

Welcome to the study "Gamble with your friends"!


Today, you find yourself in a casino.
In a series of games you are interacting with two slot machines.
The slot machines look as follows:





Each slot machine tends to pay out about the same amount of reward on average during one game, but there is variability in the reward on any given play.
For example, the average reward for the bandit on the right might be 50 points, but on the first play we might see a reward of 38 points because of the variability ...
... on the second play we might get 56 points ...
... if we open a third box on the right we might get 51 points this time ...
... and so on, such that if we were to play the right bandit 10 times in a row we might see these rewards ...

Both slot machines will have the same kind of variability and this variability will stay constant throughout the experiment.
One of the bandits will always have a higher average reward and hence is the better option to choose on average.

Instructions How To Respond In The Task

A given trial is separated into three phases:

1. An instructed phase during which we will tell you which machine to play.
This will give you some experience with each option before you make your first choice.
The respective machine is highlighted with a green square containing a question mark (?) on top of the slot machine to be chosen.

2. A memory recall phase. Occasionally, a friend who wants to play with you comes along.
She has no information about the two slot machines and asks you about the rewards you have received so far from the two machines. Try to help her!
Sometimes, you just play yourself.
That is, the phase, in which you recall the rewards to help your friend, is skipped in some trials and you are directly confronted with phase 3.

3. A choice phase during which you choose yourself between the two slot machines.
There are going to be squares with question marks (?) highlighted in green above both slot machines. You can choose any of the two machines.


To respond in part 1 / the instructed phase: click on the highlighted square with a question mark in green.

To respond in part 2 / the memory recall phase: click with the mouse into the text box appearing for this purpose.
Recall the values that you received from the slot machine, which is highlighted with a green square containing a question mark (?).
Type in your responses using the number keys on the keyboard when you see the cursor in the textbox.
Separate individual responses with a comma (,).
Note. The order of your responses is irrelvant. Type them in in any order they come to your mind.

To make your choice in part 3 / the choice phase: click on one of the two highlighted squres with a question mark in green.
Remember that you want to maximize your rewards on every trial!
